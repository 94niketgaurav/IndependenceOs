THE RUNNING EXAMPLE (used throughout this entire document)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  DOCTOR:   Dr. Priya Patel, primary care physician, independent practice
  PATIENT:  Maria Chen, 58-year-old female, Type 2 diabetes
  CLINIC:   Patel Family Medicine, 10-provider independent practice
  DATE:     March 2026

Maria has been a patient at this clinic for 11 years. She is on the clinic's
roster with Blue Cross Blue Shield Medicare Advantage — a plan that pays the
clinic bonuses based on quality scores (called Value-Based Care, or VBC).

We will follow what the system does for Maria — before her appointment,
during it, after it, and all the way until her open care gaps are closed.

================================================================================
SECTION 0 — WHY DOES THIS SYSTEM EXIST?
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE CORE PROBLEM (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Imagine you are a doctor running a busy practice. You see 18 patients a
day. You have 4,000 active patients on your panel. Your job is not just
to treat whoever walks in — your job is also to make sure every single
one of those 4,000 patients is receiving the preventive care they need.

That means:
  - Every diabetic patient should have an A1C blood test every 12 months
  - Every diabetic patient should have an annual eye exam
  - Women 50-74 should have a mammogram every 2 years
  - Adults 45-75 should have colorectal cancer screening
  - Adults 18+ should get their annual flu shot
  - And so on — ~90 different rules for different patient types

A CARE GAP is the space between what a patient SHOULD have received
and what they ACTUALLY received.

Maria Chen has Type 2 diabetes. She last had a diabetic eye exam in
February 2025. It is now March 2026. She is 13 months overdue.
That is a care gap.

The problem: How does a clinic with 4,000 patients know that Maria
specifically is overdue? How do they tell her? How do they make sure
it actually gets done — and confirm it got done?

Without a system: The doctor might notice during Maria's visit.
Or might not. Patients fall through the cracks constantly.

With this system:
  - The gap is identified automatically
  - Maria is contacted proactively (before she even books an appointment)
  - Dr. Patel sees the gap alert before the visit starts
  - When the eye exam happens, the gap is marked closed automatically
  - The clinic's quality score improves — which affects their revenue

WHY REVENUE MATTERS HERE:

Under Value-Based Care (VBC) contracts, insurance companies pay clinics
BONUSES if they hit quality targets. For a 10-provider independent practice
like Dr. Patel's, a single percentage point improvement in quality scores
can be worth $50,000 to $200,000 per year in bonus payments.

Maria's eye exam is not just good medicine. It is also worth money to the
clinic — and the system knows exactly which patients are worth the most to
track down.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The care gap system cannot determine what Maria is missing unless it first
knows what Maria HAS received. That information is scattered across multiple
separate systems:

  THE EHR (Electronic Health Record):
  This is the clinic's own records system — every visit, diagnosis,
  lab order, and prescription placed at this clinic. If Maria got her
  A1C test at THIS clinic, the EHR knows about it.

  PAYER CLAIMS:
  Insurance companies see every claim from EVERY provider, not just
  this clinic. If Maria got her eye exam at a specialist's office
  across town, the clinic's EHR doesn't know — but Blue Cross Blue
  Shield does, because they paid the specialist's bill.
  This data arrives as insurance claims (standardized files called
  837/835 EDI records). Lag time: 14 to 90 days after the service.

  STATE IMMUNIZATION REGISTRY (IIS — Immunization Information System):
  Every flu shot given anywhere in Illinois — at pharmacies, urgent
  care clinics, schools, the county health department — is reported
  to the state IIS. If Maria got her flu shot at Walgreens, the IIS
  knows. The clinic can query it.

  HIE (Health Information Exchange):
  A shared database where hospitals and health systems in a region
  post clinical data. If Maria went to Northwestern Hospital for a
  colonoscopy, that result can appear in the HIE and be retrieved here.

  LAB GATEWAY:
  Direct electronic connections to Quest Diagnostics, LabCorp, and
  in-house labs. Lab results arrive as HL7 ORU messages — standardized
  electronic lab reports.

---

FOR MARIA: The system ingests overnight:
  - EHR: Maria's last 11 years of visits at this clinic
  - Payer Claims (BCBS): 2 years of claims (her last eye exam, every
    A1C test, her last mammogram)
  - State IIS: Her flu vaccination history
  - Lab Gateway: Direct A1C results from Quest

The system now has a more complete picture of Maria's health history
than any single doctor could reconstruct from memory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Each data source connects via a different protocol:
  - EHR: FHIR R4 bidirectional sync (near real-time)
  - Payer Claims: 837/835 EDI file parsing + payer API (14-90 day lag)
  - State IIS: SOAP/REST API + weekly batch export
  - HIE: FHIR R4, IHE XDS, or HL7 ADT/ORU messages
  - Lab Gateway: HL7 ORU v2.x messages (hours)

All incoming records flow first to PATIENT IDENTITY RESOLUTION — the
critical step described in Section 2. Nothing touches Maria's profile
until the system is certain the record actually belongs to Maria.

SECTION 2 — STAGE 2: PATIENT IDENTITY RESOLUTION (EMPI)
  [Diagram nodes: Patient Identity Resolution →
   Longitudinal Patient Profile (matched)
   Patient Identity Resolution → Data Staging Queue (unmatched, dashed red)
   Data Staging Queue → Patient Identity Resolution (retry, dashed red)]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Here is the most dangerous problem in the entire system:

What if the payer claims file contains a record for a DIFFERENT Maria Chen?

There are 47,000 Maria Chens in the United States. A 58-year-old diabetic
named Maria Chen who lives in Chicago could easily be confused with another
Maria Chen in the same city.

If the system accidentally attaches ANOTHER patient's eye exam result to
OUR Maria Chen, it will close her care gap. The system thinks: "Eye exam
done, all good." But OUR Maria never got the exam. She goes another year
without anyone detecting her diabetic retinopathy.

This is the "silent false closure" problem — the most dangerous failure
mode in the entire system.

To prevent this, every incoming record goes through PATIENT IDENTITY
RESOLUTION before it can touch any patient's profile. Think of it as
a bouncer checking IDs at the door.

---

THE FOUR TRUST LEVELS:

  LEVEL 1 — AUTOMATIC MATCH, NO REVIEW NEEDED:
  The clinic already told us "this Blue Cross member ID #BC-4521-9988
  is our patient Maria Chen, MRN 10042." That mapping was made explicitly
  when the clinic enrolled Maria in the system. Perfect certainty.
  → Record attached automatically.

  LEVEL 2 — HIGH CONFIDENCE, AUTOMATIC, LOGGED:
  The record shows: DOB 1967-09-14, Female, Last name "Chen," first
  name "Maria," zip code 60614. All five fields match Maria's profile
  exactly (last name similarity ≥ 95% by Jaro-Winkler scoring — an
  algorithm that measures how close two strings are).
  → Record attached automatically. Match logged for audit.

  LEVEL 3 — PROBABLE MATCH, HUMAN REVIEW REQUIRED:
  The record shows: DOB 1967-09-14, Female, Last name "Chen," but
  first name "M." and zip code is 60615 (one digit off from 60614).
  Three of five fields match above threshold — probably the same
  person, but not certain enough to auto-attach.
  → Record goes to STAGING QUEUE.
  → Evidence HELD. Does NOT touch Maria's profile until a human
    (a care coordinator) reviews and confirms the link.

  LEVEL 4 — NO MATCH:
  The record has no demographic overlap with anyone in the system.
  → Staged indefinitely. Searchable manually.

---

FOR MARIA: Most of her records match at Level 1 or Level 2. Her BCBS
claims are Level 1 (explicitly mapped). Her Quest lab results are
Level 2 (exact DOB + last name match). One HIE record from a hospital
visit 8 years ago is Level 3 — it has her DOB but her name is listed as
"Mary Chen" (a transcription error from the hospital's old system). That
one gets flagged for a care coordinator to review before it's attached.

---

WHAT ABOUT THE EDGE CASE?

  Q: "What if there really ARE two patients named Maria Chen in this
     practice?"

  A: Each internal patient has a unique UUID (a permanent internal ID).
     The system never confuses them by name alone. Matching uses
     demographic combinations, not names in isolation. Two patients
     named Maria Chen would have different DOBs, different member IDs,
     different zip codes. They would correctly resolve to separate
     Longitudinal Patient Profiles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

EMPI (Enterprise Master Patient Index): The identity resolution service.
Implements FHIR $Patient.match operation for standardized API access.

Matching algorithm:
  - Level 1: Explicit cross-reference table (clinic_id, EHR MRN, payer member ID)
  - Level 2: Jaro-Winkler string similarity ≥ 0.95 on last name + exact DOB + sex
  - Level 3: 3-of-5 field fuzzy match (DOB, sex, first name, last name, zip)
  - Level 4: No qualifying match

All links are stored in PatientIdentityLink records:
  - trust_level: 1, 2, or 3
  - approved_by: required for Level 3 (human reviewer UUID)
  - revocable: False for Level 1-2, True for Level 3

LONGITUDINAL PATIENT PROFILE: The unified view of one patient's health
history after all matched evidence is assembled. This is the input to
the Measure Evaluation Engine.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Now the system has Maria's complete health history assembled. The next
question is: What is she SUPPOSED to have had, and does the record show
she received it?

This is where HEDIS quality measures come in.

HEDIS stands for Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set.
It is a set of ~90 standardized rules published annually by NCQA
(National Committee for Quality Assurance). Think of HEDIS as the
official rulebook for "what counts as good preventive care."

Each HEDIS measure defines:
  - Who is ELIGIBLE (the denominator — which patients does this apply to?)
  - Who is EXCLUDED (certain patients are exempt for medical reasons)
  - What counts as COMPLETING the measure (the numerator — what service
    was received, and in what time window?)

The system evaluates Maria against ALL 90 measures, in three phases:

---

PHASE 1 — DENOMINATOR EVALUATION (Is Maria eligible for this measure?)

  EXAMPLE: Diabetic Eye Exam (HEDIS measure "CDC-Eye")
  Rule: "Patients 18-75 with Type 2 diabetes, enrolled in this plan
         for at least 11 months this year."
  Maria: 58 years old, has Type 2 diabetes (ICD-10 code E11.9 in her
         record), enrolled in BCBS Medicare Advantage for 12 months.
  Result: Maria IS in the denominator. She is eligible.

  EXAMPLE: Breast Cancer Screening (HEDIS measure "BCS-E")
  Rule: "Women 50-74 enrolled for 11+ months."
  Maria: 58 years old, female, enrolled 12 months.
  Result: Maria IS eligible for breast cancer screening too.

---

PHASE 2 — EXCLUSION EVALUATION (Is Maria exempt?)

  This phase asks: Is there a medical reason Maria should NOT receive
  this service?

  EXAMPLE: Bilateral mastectomy patients are excluded from breast
  cancer screening. There is no point sending them for a mammogram.

  IMPORTANT: Exclusions are evaluated INDEPENDENTLY per measure.
  A patient in hospice is excluded from ALL measures (no point in
  preventive care for someone in end-of-life care). But a patient
  who had a mastectomy is only excluded from the breast cancer
  measure — NOT from diabetic eye exams or colorectal screening.

  FOR MARIA: She has no exclusions on file for any measure. She passes
  Phase 2 for all measures she was eligible for.

---

PHASE 3 — NUMERATOR EVALUATION (Has Maria met the measure?)

  This phase asks: Did she actually get the service?

  EXAMPLE: Diabetic Eye Exam
  Rule: "Retinal or dilated eye exam performed in the last 12 months."
  Code sets checked: specific CPT codes for retinal exams
  Data sources checked: EHR procedures, payer claims, HIE
  Maria's record: Last retinal exam — February 12, 2025.
  Today: March 15, 2026.
  That is 13 months ago. Outside the 12-month window.
  Result: CARE GAP OPEN.

  EXAMPLE: A1C Test (HEDIS measure "CDC-HbA1c")
  Rule: "A1C test performed in the last 12 months."
  Maria's record: Last A1C — October 3, 2025. (5 months ago)
  Result: NOT a gap. A1C is current. No action needed.

---

WHAT ABOUT ANNUAL UPDATES?

Each HEDIS measure is stored as a VERSIONED SPECIFICATION with an
effective date. When NCQA releases updated rules each October, the
system loads the new spec rows with effective_date = next January 1.
Historical evaluations still use the spec that was current at the time.
No code deployment needed — updating the rules is a database update.

If an insurance company has slightly different requirements than the
HEDIS standard (Aetna might require annual mammograms instead of
biennial), those are stored as PAYER OVERRIDES that inherit from the
base spec and change only the specific fields that differ.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

MEASURE SPEC STORE: Versioned MeasureSpec rows (measure_id, hedis_year,
effective_date, denominator rules, exclusion code sets, numerator code
sets and lookback windows). PayerMeasureOverlay rows for payer-specific
modifications.

THREE-PHASE EVALUATION runs per patient × measure combination:
  Phase 1 (Denominator): Age/sex check, enrollment months, qualifying
                         diagnosis codes for condition-based measures
  Phase 2 (Exclusion): Diagnosis code set lookup, procedure code lookup,
                       manual exclusion registry, hospice enrollment check
  Phase 3 (Numerator): Service code lookup across all data sources
                       within lookback window; result check if required

MEASURE EVALUATION is STATELESS and PARALLELIZABLE — each patient-measure
pair can be evaluated independently on separate workers.

RECALCULATION TRIGGER MODEL (dirty-flag priority queue):
  NOT every patient re-evaluated every night (too slow at scale).
  Instead:
    - Data event arrives → set needs_recalculation=true on patient profile
    - Tier 1 (HIGH priority): likely closure event → process within 15 min
    - Tier 2 (STANDARD): new data → process within 24 hours
    - Nightly batch: patients with no recent events → rolling 30-day refresh
    - January 1: full population recalculation for annual measure reset

WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A care gap has been found for Maria: her diabetic eye exam is overdue.
The system now creates a formal GAP RECORD for this finding. Think of
a gap record as a case file that tracks everything about this specific
gap from discovery to closure.

The gap record for Maria includes:
  - Patient: Maria Chen (UUID)
  - Measure: CDC-Eye (Diabetic Eye Exam)
  - HEDIS Year: 2026
  - Payer: BCBS Medicare Advantage
  - Status: OPEN
  - Opened: March 15, 2026
  - Last completed: February 12, 2025
  - Evidence list: empty (nothing recent enough to close it)
  - Priority Score: calculated next

---

NOT ALL GAPS ARE EQUAL — PRIORITY SCORING:

The clinic has 4,000 patients. There are probably hundreds of open gaps
across the panel. They cannot call every patient today. The system must
decide: Who needs to be reached FIRST?

Priority Score uses four factors, each weighted:

  1. CLINICAL URGENCY (40% of score):
     How dangerous is it that this went undone?
     Maria's eye exam: 13 months overdue for a diabetic patient.
     Untreated diabetic retinopathy can cause permanent blindness.
     Clinical urgency: HIGH (score: 85/100)

  2. VBC FINANCIAL IMPACT (35% of score):
     Is this patient on a contract that pays the clinic a bonus for
     closing this gap? How close is the clinic to the bonus threshold?
     Maria is on BCBS Medicare Advantage, which has a VBC contract
     that specifically weighs diabetic eye exams. The clinic is 3
     patients away from crossing the 80% threshold for Tier 2 bonus
     ($47,500 bonus at stake). Maria's closure would count toward that.
     Financial impact: HIGH (score: 92/100)

  3. OUTREACH RECEPTIVENESS (15% of score):
     Does the system have good contact info? Has she responded before?
     Maria has a verified cell phone and has replied to SMS messages
     before. Her preferred language is English (templates available).
     Receptiveness: HIGH (score: 88/100)

  4. PRACTICE CONTRACT WEIGHT (10% of score):
     Is this specific measure heavily weighted in the contract?
     Diabetic eye exam is in the BCBS MA Quality Incentive Program.
     Contract weight: HIGH (score: 90/100)

  FINAL PRIORITY SCORE:
  (85 × 0.40) + (92 × 0.35) + (88 × 0.15) + (90 × 0.10)
  = 34 + 32.2 + 13.2 + 9
  = 88.4 out of 100
  → Maria's eye exam gap is HIGH priority.

---

PROVIDER GAP SUMMARY (the highest-ROI feature):

The gap record feeds TWO things simultaneously:

  1. The outreach engine (described in Section 5)
  2. The PROVIDER GAP SUMMARY — a real-time summary shown to Dr. Patel
     right before Maria's next visit

When Maria books a wellness exam appointment, the care gap system
pushes a pre-encounter alert to the Medical Scribe interface:

  ╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
  ║  OPEN CARE GAPS — MARIA CHEN (DOB: 1967-09-14)              ║
  ║══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════║
  ║  🔴 OVERDUE 13 months: Diabetic Eye Exam (last: Feb 2025)   ║
  ║                         [Refer to ophthalmology →]           ║
  ║  🟡 DUE THIS MONTH:    Flu Vaccine (not yet this season)    ║
  ║                         [Order flu shot →]                   ║

Dr. Patel sees this before she walks in the door. In-visit gap closure
rates are 3 to 5 times higher than outreach-driven closure — because
the doctor can place the referral right now while Maria is sitting there.

WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

GAP RECORD STORE: PostgreSQL table with CareGap rows:
  gap_id, patient_id, clinic_id, measure_id, hedis_year, payer_id,
  status (OPEN/IN_PROGRESS/CLOSED/EXCLUDED/PATIENT_DECLINED),
  opened_at, closed_at, not_due_until, priority_score,
  evidence (list, append-only), outreach_campaign reference,
  exclusion_reason.

PRIORITY SCORER:
  priority_score = (clinical_urgency × 0.40)
                 + (vbc_financial_impact × 0.35)
                 + (outreach_receptiveness × 0.15)
                 + (practice_contract_weight × 0.10)
  Each sub-score is 0-100. Final score is 0-100.

VBC FINANCIAL REPORT feeds current-contract-performance context into
Priority Scorer so patients that would push the clinic over a bonus
threshold are elevated.

PROVIDER GAP SUMMARY: Called via GapSummaryAPI at encounter creation.
Returns top N open gaps sorted by priority score. Integration point
with Medical Scribe system.

SECTION 5 — STAGE 5: OUTREACH ENGINE
  [Diagram nodes: Outreach Campaign Manager → Channel Dispatcher →
   SMS Gateway / Patient Portal Message / Automated Voice / Letter Service →
   Outreach Response Handler]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Maria has a high-priority open gap. She hasn't booked an appointment yet.
So the system needs to REACH HER to let her know.

But it can't just blast a message to everyone. There are rules:

  TCPA COMPLIANCE: The Telephone Consumer Protection Act requires
  explicit written consent before sending automated SMS messages.
  If Maria has not opted in to SMS from this clinic, the system
  CANNOT text her, no matter how urgent the gap is.

  PATIENT PREFERENCES: If Maria has told the clinic she prefers
  the patient portal over phone calls, the system should respect that.

  LANGUAGE: If Maria's preferred language is Mandarin, the message
  needs to be in Mandarin. If the clinic doesn't have a Mandarin
  template for this message, they should call with an interpreter
  instead of sending a confusing English text.

---

FOR MARIA: She opted in to SMS when she registered. She has a
verified cell phone. English is her primary language. The system
decides to start with SMS.

Day 1 — Maria receives this text from the clinic:

  "Hi Maria — Dr. Patel's office here. We noticed you're due for
   your annual diabetic eye exam (it helps catch early vision
   problems from diabetes). We can refer you to an eye specialist
   — no appointment at our office needed. Would you like us to
   send the referral? Reply YES or call us at (312) 555-0100.
   Reply STOP to opt out."

This message was GENERATED by Claude (the AI), not written by a
staff member. It uses Maria's name, the specific measure, a plain
English explanation of why it matters, and a clear action.
It is written at a 6th-grade reading level.

---

THE CAMPAIGN SEQUENCE (if Maria doesn't respond):

  Day 1:   SMS
  Day 7:   Patient portal message (or SMS again if portal not opened)
  Day 21:  Automated voice call
  Day 42:  Mailed letter
  Day 90:  Manual review queue — a care coordinator calls personally

Each step is tracked. If Maria replies "STOP" at any point, all
SMS messages stop immediately (TCPA compliance — immediate opt-out).

---

WHAT IF MARIA SAYS NO?

  Maria replies: "No thanks, not interested."
  → Status: PATIENT_DECLINED
  → The system does NOT re-send outreach for this gap this year
  → A care coordinator reviews whether a clinical contraindication
    exists (has Maria been told by an ophthalmologist she doesn't
    need the exam?)
  → If contraindication confirmed: gap → EXCLUDED

WHAT IF MARIA MAKES AN APPOINTMENT BUT DOESN'T SHOW?

  The appointment no-show does NOT close the campaign.
  After 7 days, the campaign re-activates with a gentler follow-up.
  After TWO no-shows on the same gap, the campaign escalates to a
  personal phone call from a care coordinator — not an automated one.

WHAT IF ALL ATTEMPTS FAIL?

  After Day 90 with no response: OUTREACH EXHAUSTED.
  The case goes to a MANUAL REVIEW QUEUE for human follow-up.
  The care coordinator may know that Maria is traveling abroad for
  6 months, or that she is a hard-to-reach patient who needs a
  different approach entirely.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

OUTREACH CAMPAIGN MANAGER: State machine per patient-gap pair.
  States: PENDING → OUTREACH_SCHEDULED → CONTACTED
    → APPOINTMENT_MADE → SERVICE_COMPLETED → (triggers gap closure)
    → PATIENT_DECLINED → (exclusion workflow)
    → NO_RESPONSE → next channel attempt
    → OUTREACH_EXHAUSTED → manual review queue

CHANNEL DISPATCHER: Priority logic:
  1. Check TCPA opt-outs (if SMS opted-out → skip SMS)
  2. Check patient preferences (if portal preferred → start with portal)
  3. Check contact data confidence (phone confidence < 0.7 → skip phone)
  4. Check language (template exists for preferred_language?)
  5. Clinical urgency: if HIGH and portal not opened in 30 days → phone
  6. Concurrent outreach lock: if payer program also outreaching → hold

RESPONSE HANDLER parses SMS responses automatically:
  "YES", "yes", "y", "ok", "sure" → appointment scheduling / lab order
  "STOP", "stop", "unsubscribe"   → immediate opt-out
  "NO", "no", "not interested"    → patient declined workflow
  Anything else                   → route to front desk

LLM MESSAGE GENERATION: Claude generates personalized messages using:
  patient name, specific measure, clinical explanation, call to action.
  Literacy target: 6th-grade reading level. Translated if needed.

WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Maria replies "YES" to the SMS. The system routes this to the
APPOINTMENT REQUEST SERVICE.

There are two ways this can go:

  OPTION A — CLINIC REFERRAL:
  The system sends a referral request to the ophthalmology specialist.
  An appointment is scheduled. The campaign status moves to
  APPOINTMENT_MADE.

  OPTION B — LAB ORDER (for tests, not specialist referrals):
  For something like an A1C test, the system can create a standing
  lab order so Maria can walk into any Quest or LabCorp location
  without booking anything specific. The gap moves to IN_PROGRESS
  (service pending).

---

WHAT IF MARIA DOESN'T SHOW?

  The ophthalmologist reports a no-show. The appointment completion
  status is NOT updated. After 24 hours with no status update,
  the NO SHOW HANDLER fires:

  "Looks like Maria's appointment didn't happen. Re-activating her
  campaign in 7 days with a follow-up message."

  After a SECOND no-show, the system escalates to a care coordinator
  personal call — because two no-shows usually means there is a barrier
  (transportation issue, fear, cost concern, didn't understand what
  the exam involved).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

APPOINTMENT REQUEST SERVICE:
  - Sends referral to specialist EHR via FHIR $referral operation
  - Or creates lab order via EHR API for walk-in lab visits
  - Sets gap status → IN_PROGRESS (suppresses further outreach)

NO SHOW HANDLER:
  - Triggered when appointment status not updated within 24 hours
    of scheduled appointment time
  - Sets campaign status → NO_RESPONSE with "appointment_missed" note
  - Schedules next campaign step in 7 days
  - After 2nd no-show: escalates to MANUAL_REVIEW with "escalated" flag

------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Appointment Request Service ─ ─► No Show Handler (dashed red, no-show)
  No Show Handler ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─► Outreach Campaign Manager

SECTION 7 — STAGE 7: CLOSURE CONFIRMATION
  [Diagram nodes: Note Signed Event → Gap Evidence Monitor
   Claim Paid Event → Gap Evidence Monitor
   Gap Evidence Monitor → Numerator Evaluator (re-evaluate)
   Gap Evidence Monitor → Pending Result Tracker
   Provider Attestation → Gap Evidence Monitor
   Pending Result Tracker → Pending Result Timeout (dashed red)
   Pending Result Timeout → Gap Record Store (reopen, dashed red)
   Pending Result Timeout → Manual Review Queue (dashed red)]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Maria went to Dr. Nguyen (the ophthalmologist). He performed a dilated
retinal exam. Now how does the care gap system KNOW the exam was done?

There are FIVE ways a gap can be confirmed closed:

  SOURCE 1 — EHR NOTE SIGNED (fastest):
  The Medical Scribe system fires a "NoteSignedEvent" when Dr. Patel
  (or any provider in the practice) signs a note containing relevant
  codes. If Dr. Patel herself places the referral and documents it,
  the gap can move to IN_PROGRESS within minutes.

  SOURCE 2 — CLAIM PAID (authoritative, but slowest):
  When BCBS pays Dr. Nguyen's claim for the eye exam, a
  "ClaimPaidEvent" is published. This is the insurance company's
  confirmation that the service happened and was accepted.
  The care gap system receives this and closes the gap.
  Timeline: 14 to 90 days after the service.

  SOURCE 3 — LAB RESULT (for test-based measures):
  If the A1C result arrives directly from Quest via HL7 message,
  the gap evidence monitor receives it and routes it to the
  Numerator Evaluator for immediate re-evaluation.

  SOURCE 4 — HIE (for services at other facilities):
  If Maria had her eye exam at a hospital eye clinic connected to
  the HIE, the procedure record arrives and triggers closure.

  SOURCE 5 — PROVIDER ATTESTATION (for services outside the system):
  Dr. Patel's nurse calls Maria and learns she actually got the eye
  exam in Taiwan during a family trip — no US insurance claim, no
  US HIE record. The nurse can enter a PROVIDER ATTESTATION:
  "Patient self-reported dilated retinal exam on December 15, 2025,
  at Taipei Veterans General Hospital. Paper record on file."
  The attestation closes the gap under HEDIS hybrid method.
  (HEDIS allows medical record evidence to stand even without claims.)

---

THE PENDING RESULT TRACKER:

Sometimes the system knows a service was ordered but hasn't received
confirmation yet. For example:
  - Dr. Patel orders an A1C today
  - The lab draws blood tomorrow
  - Results arrive in 48 hours

During this window:
  Gap status → IN_PROGRESS (outreach suppressed — no point texting
  Maria about her A1C when she already has a pending order)

BUT: What if the lab loses the specimen? What if Maria never went?
The system has TIMEOUTS per test type:
  - A1C: wait 48 hours
  - Colonoscopy: wait 14 days (pathology report expected)
  - Mammogram: wait 5 business days

If the result does not arrive within the timeout:
  Gap status → OPEN (reopened)
  Provider notified: "Expected A1C result for Maria Chen not received"
  Outreach does NOT automatically restart — provider reviews first

---

IMPORTANT RULE — A DENIED CLAIM DOES NOT REOPEN THE GAP:

If Dr. Nguyen's claim gets denied by BCBS (billing error — wrong CPT
code, missing modifier), the care gap system IGNORES this denial.
A ClaimDeniedEvent means a billing mistake — it does NOT mean the
eye exam didn't happen. If a NoteSignedEvent or HIE record already
confirmed the exam, the gap stays CLOSED.

This is a critical difference: Claim Scrubbing cares about billing.
Care Gap cares about whether the service was performed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

GAP EVIDENCE MONITOR: Subscribes to:
  - NoteSignedEvent (from Medical Scribe system)
  - ClaimPaidEvent (from Claim Scrubbing system)
  - HL7 lab results (from Lab Gateway)
  - HIE procedure events
  - Provider attestation submissions

Each incoming evidence piece is stored as a GapEvidence record:
  evidence_id, gap_id, evidence_type, source_id, service_date,
  cpt_code, loinc_code, result_value, confidence, received_at,
  applied_to_numerator

GapEvidence is APPEND-ONLY (never updated or deleted — full audit trail).

NUMERATOR EVALUATOR RE-RUN: triggered by Gap Evidence Monitor.
Same three-phase evaluation; now with new evidence attached.
If numerator satisfied → gap status → CLOSED.

PENDING RESULT TRACKER: Stores expected_result_by timestamp per test.
Timeout job runs every 6 hours to catch overdue results.

PROVIDER ATTESTATION: Manual form stored as GapEvidence with
evidence_type = PROVIDER_ATTESTATION, approved_by = attestor UUID.
Satisfies HEDIS hybrid method.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Claim Paid Event  ────────────────► Gap Evidence Monitor

SECTION 8 — STAGE 8: QUALITY REPORTING
  [Diagram nodes: Gap Record Store → Quality Reporting Engine →
   HEDIS Submission / Population Dashboard / VBC Financial Report]
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (plain English):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every time a gap closes (or opens, or is excluded), the GAP RECORD STORE
is updated. The QUALITY REPORTING ENGINE reads from this store to answer
three different reporting needs:

  1. HEDIS SUBMISSION:
  At year-end, insurance companies require the clinic to submit a
  formal report showing: "Of your eligible patients, what percentage
  met each quality measure?" This report goes to NCQA (the national
  accreditation body) and to each payer.
  The system generates this automatically in the required format.
  For HEDIS Hybrid method, it includes attestation records alongside
  claims data.

  2. POPULATION DASHBOARD:
  A real-time view for the clinic's quality team showing:
  - How many patients have each gap open right now?
  - Which measures are the clinic scoring lowest on?
  - How have closure rates changed month-over-month?
  - Which outreach campaigns are working (high response) vs. not?

  3. VBC FINANCIAL REPORT:
  The financial translation layer — takes quality performance and
  converts it to dollar projections:

    "Current diabetic eye exam rate: 74% of eligible patients.
     BCBS Tier 2 threshold: 80%.
     Patients needed: 6 more closures.
     Estimated bonus at Tier 2: $47,500.
     Revenue per eye exam closure: ~$7,917.
     Top 6 patients by priority score: [list]"

  This report tells practice managers where to focus their effort —
  not just clinically, but financially.

---

FOR MARIA: When her eye exam closes, the Quality Reporting Engine:
  - Increments the CDC-Eye numerator count for BCBS MA by 1
  - Recalculates the clinic's CDC-Eye measure rate: now 75%
  - Updates the VBC financial projection
  - If this pushes the clinic to 80% → triggers bonus tier notification

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS HAPPENING (technically):
------------------------------------------------------------------------

QUALITY REPORTING ENGINE:
  - HEDIS Administrative method: uses claims + EHR procedure data only
  - HEDIS Hybrid method: adds medical record attestations
  - Output format: NCQA-specified XML/CSV per payer
  - Medicare Advantage: Star Ratings CMS format
  - VBC contracts: Custom P4P scorecards per contract

POPULATION DASHBOARD: Materialized view updated after each gap status
change. Aggregates by clinic_id, measure_id, hedis_year.

VBC FINANCIAL REPORT: Generated on-demand per clinic, cached 24 hours.
Priority target list: patients sorted by priority_score descending,
filtered to gaps on VBC-contract measures where practice is near threshold.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT YOU SEE IN THE DIAGRAM:
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Gap Record Store ──────────────► Quality Reporting Engine
  Quality Reporting Engine ──────► HEDIS Submission
  Quality Reporting Engine ──────► Population Dashboard
  Quality Reporting Engine ──────► VBC Financial Report

  (VBC Financial Report also feeds back to Priority Scorer —

SECTION 9 — EDGE CASES (When things don't go as planned)
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EDGE CASE 1: Two patients named Maria Chen
------------------------------------------------------------------------

The EMPI (identity resolution system) never matches by name alone.
It requires DOB + sex + last name + additional fields. Two patients
with the same name but different DOBs resolve to separate profiles.
Their gap records are entirely independent.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EDGE CASE 2: Maria opts out of SMS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Maria replies STOP. The system immediately opts her out of all SMS
campaigns. The current SMS campaign halts. But her Letter Service
step (already queued for Day 42) can still be sent — it is a different
channel and not subject to TCPA's electronic messaging rules.
The next outreach attempt uses the Patient Portal or phone.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EDGE CASE 3: Maria's insurance doesn't require this measure
------------------------------------------------------------------------

If Maria switches to a fee-for-service plan (no VBC contract), the
HEDIS measure still runs, but:
  - VBC Financial Impact score drops to near zero
  - Practice Contract Weight drops to zero
  - Priority score drops significantly
  - Outreach still happens (it is still good medicine) but is lower priority
  Gap records are maintained per payer overlay — one gap per
  measure-payer combination. Her standard HEDIS gap and her BCBS
  MA contract gap are tracked independently.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EDGE CASE 4: HEDIS updates its rules next January
------------------------------------------------------------------------

NCQA releases updated HEDIS specifications each October.
The update pipeline:
  1. Ingest NCQA specification release (PDF → structured data)
  2. Generate spec diff (what changed?)
  3. Human review gate (quality team approves changes)
  4. Load new MeasureSpec rows with effective_date = Jan 1 next year
  5. On Jan 1: new specs activate; population re-evaluates
No code deployment needed. Historical gaps evaluated under the spec
that was active at the time they were evaluated.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
EDGE CASE 5: Eye exam done by a specialist outside this practice
------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the most common case — specialty care at a separate office.
Three ways the closure gets detected:

  a) Payer claim: BCBS pays the ophthalmologist's claim. ClaimPaidEvent
     arrives 14-90 days later. Gap closes.

  b) HIE: If the ophthalmologist's system participates in the regional
     HIE, the procedure record arrives faster (hours to days).

  c) Patient or doctor attestation: Maria calls the clinic to report
     the exam. Staff enters a Provider Attestation. Gap closes immediately
     under HEDIS hybrid method.

This is why having multiple closure evidence sources matters — relying
on payer claims alone means 90-day delays in closing gaps that are
already done.

SECTION 12 — THE THREE MOST IMPORTANT DESIGN RULES
================================================================================

If you remember nothing else from this document, remember these three:

  1. IDENTITY RESOLUTION MUST BE CORRECT BEFORE ANY EVIDENCE IS APPLIED.
     A false patient identity match that attaches another patient's
     eye exam result to Maria silently closes her gap. She never gets
     follow-up. This is the most dangerous failure mode in the system.
     That is why Level 3 (probabilistic) matches require human review
     before evidence is applied — the cost of false closure exceeds
     the cost of delay.

  2. THE PRE-ENCOUNTER SUMMARY IS THE HIGHEST-ROI FEATURE.
     In-visit gap closure rates are 3 to 5 times higher than
     outreach-driven closure. When a doctor can order the A1C test,
     administer the flu shot, or place the eye exam referral while the
     patient is physically in the room, it happens immediately. Every
     other feature in this system exists to support and supplement this
     one moment. If you can only build one thing, build the pre-encounter
     summary first.

  3. A DENIED CLAIM DOES NOT REOPEN A CLOSED GAP.
     Claim denial is a billing problem — it means a CPT code was wrong
     or a modifier was missing. It does NOT mean the medical service
     did not happen. If a NoteSignedEvent or lab result or HIE record
     already confirmed the service was performed, the gap stays CLOSED.
     The systems have separate concerns: Claim Scrubbing fixes the bill.
     Care Gap tracks the clinical service. Never confuse the two.
